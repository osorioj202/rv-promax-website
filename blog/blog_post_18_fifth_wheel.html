What Is a Fifth Wheel? A Complete Overview for RV Owners

A fifth wheel is a type of RV trailer that connects to a special hitch mounted in the bed of a pickup truck, rather than a traditional bumper hitch. This design offers several advantages:

  • Improved Stability: The hitch placement over the truck’s rear axle distributes weight more evenly, reducing sway and improving towing control.
  • Increased Living Space: Fifth wheels often feature bi-level floor plans, providing more headroom and spacious interiors.
  • Better Maneuverability: The pivot point in the truck bed allows for tighter turns and easier backing.
  • Higher Towing Capacity: Many heavy-duty trucks can tow larger fifth wheels compared to conventional travel trailers.

Fifth wheels come in various sizes and layouts, from compact models ideal for weekend camping to luxurious full-time living rigs.

Fifth Wheel Buying Guide: Features, Sizing, Compatibility, and Installation

Key Features to Consider

  • Weight Capacity: Match the hitch’s Gross Trailer Weight (GTW) rating to your fifth wheel’s loaded weight.
  • Adjustability: Height adjustment ensures proper leveling between truck and trailer.
  • Locking Mechanism: Secure locking systems prevent accidental uncoupling.
  • Material and Finish: Powder-coated steel resists rust and corrosion.
  • Ease of Installation: Some hitches come with installation kits or require professional setup.

Sizing and Compatibility

  • Truck Bed Size: Most fifth wheel hitches fit standard 6.5 or 8-foot truck beds.
  • Truck Towing Capacity: Verify your truck’s maximum towing and payload ratings.
  • Trailer Kingpin Height: Ensure hitch height matches trailer kingpin for level towing.

Installation Overview

  • Mount the hitch rails in the truck bed using the provided hardware.
  • Attach the hitch head to the rails.
  • Adjust height and locking mechanisms.
  • Test coupling and uncoupling with the trailer.

For detailed installation, see the step-by-step guide below.

Common Fifth Wheel Problems and Expert Solutions

Problem: Trailer Sway and Instability

Solution: Use a high-quality hitch with a tight locking mechanism. Consider adding sway control devices or weight distribution systems.

Problem: Difficulty Backing Up

Solution: Practice with a spotter and use backup cameras. Some hitches offer pivoting heads for easier maneuvering.

Problem: Hitch Noise and Vibration

Solution: Lubricate hitch components regularly and check for loose bolts.

Problem: Corrosion and Rust

Solution: Clean and inspect the hitch after exposure to moisture. Apply rust-resistant coatings as needed.

Professional Tips and Best Practices for Fifth Wheel Owners

  • Always check your truck and trailer weight ratings before towing.
  • Perform a pre-trip inspection of hitch components and connections.
  • Use a torque wrench to tighten bolts to manufacturer specifications.
  • Invest in a quality hitch lock to prevent theft.
  • Store your hitch in a dry place when not in use.
  • Regularly lubricate moving parts with appropriate grease.
  • Use wheel chocks and stabilizers when parked.

Step-by-Step Fifth Wheel Hitch Installation Guide

  1. Prepare the Truck Bed: Clean and clear the bed of debris.
  2. Install Mounting Rails: Position rails according to manufacturer specs, usually 24 inches apart center-to-center.
  3. Secure Rails: Use provided bolts and torque to recommended settings.
  4. Attach Hitch Head: Mount the hitch head to the rails and secure.
  5. Adjust Height: Set hitch height to match trailer kingpin height.
  6. Test Coupling: Lower trailer kingpin into hitch, lock securely.
  7. Safety Check: Attach safety chains, test locking mechanism.
  8. Final Inspection: Check all bolts and connections before towing.

Maintenance, Troubleshooting, and Care Instructions

  • Monthly: Inspect hitch for cracks, rust, and loose bolts.
  • Before Each Trip: Lubricate hitch jaws and pivot points.
  • After Exposure to Water: Dry and apply anti-corrosion spray.
  • Troubleshooting: If hitch locks won’t engage, check for debris or damage.
  • Storage: Remove hitch from truck bed if not in use for extended periods.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: Can I use a fifth wheel hitch in any pickup truck?
A: No, your truck must have a compatible bed size and sufficient towing capacity.

Q2: How do I know the right hitch weight capacity?
A: Match or exceed your trailer’s Gross Trailer Weight (GTW).

Q3: Is professional installation necessary?
A: Not always, but recommended if you’re unfamiliar with truck bed modifications.

Q4: How often should I lubricate my fifth wheel hitch?
A: At least once a month and before every trip.

Q5: Can I tow a fifth wheel with a short bed truck?
A: Yes, but you may need a sliding hitch to prevent cab contact during turns.

Q6: What safety features should I look for in a hitch?
A: Secure locking jaws, safety chains, and hitch locks.

Q7: How do I prevent trailer sway?
A: Use a quality hitch, maintain proper tongue weight, and consider sway control devices.

Q8: Are there weight limits for the truck bed when installing a fifth wheel hitch?
A: Yes, always check your truck’s payload rating to avoid overloading.
