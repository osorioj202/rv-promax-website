Why an RV On Demand Water Heater is Essential for Every RVer

Traditional RV water heaters rely on a tank to store hot water, which can run out quickly and waste energy by keeping water heated constantly. On demand water heaters, also called tankless water heaters, heat water instantly as it flows through the unit, providing endless hot water on demand. This means:

  • Energy efficiency: Only heats water when needed, reducing propane or electric consumption.
  • Space-saving design: Compact units free up valuable storage space.
  • Continuous hot water: No waiting for tanks to refill or reheat.
  • Lightweight: Ideal for RVs where weight matters.
  • Improved comfort: Consistent water temperature for showers and chores.

For RV owners, these benefits translate into more enjoyable trips, lower utility costs, and less hassle.

Technical Overview: How RV On Demand Water Heaters Work

An RV on demand water heater uses a heat exchanger powered by propane or electricity to heat water instantly as it passes through the unit. Key components include:

  • Flow sensor: Detects water flow to activate heating.
  • Burner or heating element: Propane burner or electric coil heats water.
  • Temperature control: Maintains consistent output temperature.
  • Safety features: Overheat protection, flame failure detection, and pressure relief valves.

Most RV models operate on 12V DC power for ignition and control, with propane or 120V AC for heating. Flow rates typically range from 1.5 to 3 gallons per minute (GPM), sufficient for showers and sinks.

Comprehensive Buying Guide: Choosing the Right RV On Demand Water Heater

Key Features to Consider

  • Fuel Type: Propane is most common for RVs due to availability and efficiency; electric models require shore power or inverter capacity.
  • Flow Rate: Measured in gallons per minute (GPM). For a single shower and sink, 1.5–2.5 GPM is sufficient. Larger rigs or multiple simultaneous uses require higher flow rates.
  • Size & Weight: Compact and lightweight units save space and reduce weight load.
  • Ignition Type: Battery, electric, or manual ignition options.
  • Temperature Control: Digital displays and adjustable thermostats improve comfort.
  • Freeze Protection: Essential for cold-weather camping.
  • Safety Features: Overheat protection, flame failure detection, and pressure relief valves.
  • Installation Compatibility: Check if the unit fits your RV’s plumbing and power systems.

Common Problems and Troubleshooting Your RV On Demand Water Heater

Problem: No Hot Water or Inconsistent Temperature

  • Check propane supply and tank levels.
  • Inspect ignition system: Replace batteries or check spark igniter.
  • Clean or replace clogged water filters.
  • Verify water flow sensor operation.
  • Check for frozen pipes or unit freeze protection activation.

Problem: Water Heater Shuts Off Mid-Use

  • Overheat protection triggered: Reduce flow rate or lower temperature setting.
  • Propane pressure too low: Check regulator and tank.
  • Ventilation issues: Ensure exhaust vents are clear.

Problem: Pilot Light Won’t Ignite

  • Battery replacement or ignition system check.
  • Clean burner and pilot assembly.
  • Check propane valve and connections.

Expert Tips and Best Practices for RV On Demand Water Heaters

  • Preheat water before showering: Run water briefly to warm pipes.
  • Use low-flow showerheads: Reduce water and energy consumption.
  • Regularly inspect propane lines and fittings for leaks.
  • Winterize your system: Drain and insulate to prevent freeze damage.
  • Install a water pressure regulator: Protect your heater from high pressure.
  • Use a water softener if in hard water areas: Prevent scale buildup.
  • Schedule annual professional maintenance: Clean burners and check safety systems.

Step-by-Step Installation Guide for RV On Demand Water Heaters

  1. Choose location: Near water source, with proper ventilation.
  2. Turn off water and power supplies.
  3. Mount unit securely: Follow manufacturer’s instructions.
  4. Connect water lines: Use flexible hoses and Teflon tape.
  5. Connect propane or electric supply: Ensure proper fittings and regulators.
  6. Install venting system: For propane units, vent exhaust safely outside.
  7. Test for leaks: Use soapy water on connections.
  8. Power on and test operation: Adjust temperature settings.
  9. Check safety devices: Pressure relief valve and flame sensors.
  10. Document installation for warranty.

Safety Note: Always follow RVIA (Recreational Vehicle Industry Association) codes and local regulations. Consider professional installation if unsure.

Maintenance and Care for Longevity

  • Flush unit every 6 months: Remove mineral deposits with vinegar or descaling solution.
  • Inspect and clean burner assembly annually.
  • Check and replace water filters regularly.
  • Winterize by draining and adding antifreeze if storing in freezing conditions.
  • Monitor for corrosion or leaks.
  • Keep venting clear of debris.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is the difference between tank and on demand water heaters for RVs?

Tank heaters store hot water and can run out; on demand heaters heat water instantly, providing endless hot water.

2. Can I install an on demand water heater myself?

Yes, if you have plumbing and electrical experience, but professional installation is recommended for safety.

3. How much propane does an on demand water heater use?

Typically 0.5 to 1.5 gallons per hour depending on flow rate and temperature settings.

4. Are electric on demand water heaters suitable for RVs?

Yes, if you have sufficient shore power or inverter capacity; they are quieter and cleaner but less common.

5. How do I winterize my on demand water heater?

Drain all water, add RV antifreeze to lines, and ensure freeze protection features are active.

6. What maintenance does an on demand water heater require?

Regular flushing, burner cleaning, filter replacement, and leak inspections.

7. Can I use an on demand water heater outdoors?

Some models are designed for outdoor use; check manufacturer specifications.

8. How long do RV on demand water heaters last?

With proper maintenance, 8–12 years is typical.

9. What flow rate do I need for my RV?

1.5–2.5 GPM for most RVs; larger rigs may require higher flow rates.

10. Are there any safety certifications I should look for?

Look for RVIA certification and compliance with propane and electrical safety standards.
