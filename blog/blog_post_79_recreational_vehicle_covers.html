Why You Need a Recreational Vehicle Cover

RV covers are essential for protecting your vehicle from environmental damage such as UV rays, rain, snow, dust, bird droppings, and tree sap. They help prevent paint fading, rust, and interior damage caused by prolonged sun exposure. For owners who store their RVs outdoors or in uncovered areas, a high-quality cover is a cost-effective way to extend the life of your investment.

Types of RV Covers: Which One Is Right for You?

  • Indoor Covers: Lightweight, dust-resistant covers designed for garages or covered storage.
  • Outdoor Covers: Heavy-duty, weatherproof covers that protect against rain, snow, and UV rays.
  • Custom-Fit Covers: Tailored to your RV’s exact dimensions for maximum protection.
  • Universal Covers: Adjustable covers that fit a range of RV sizes and types.
  • Seasonal Covers: Specialized for winter or summer conditions, offering enhanced insulation or breathability.

Material Breakdown: Durability, Breathability, and Protection

  • Polypropylene: Lightweight and breathable, ideal for mild climates.
  • Polyester with UV Coating: Offers excellent UV protection and durability.
  • PVC or Vinyl: Waterproof but less breathable; best for heavy rain or snow.
  • Multi-Layer Laminates: Combine breathability with weather resistance for all-season use.

How to Measure Your RV for the Perfect Cover Fit

  1. Measure the length from front to back, including bumpers.
  2. Measure the height from ground to the highest point.
  3. Measure the width at the widest point.
  4. Check for accessories like antennas or roof racks that may require extra space.
  5. Consult manufacturer sizing charts to select the correct cover size.

Installation Tips: How to Properly Use Your RV Cover

  • Clean your RV before covering to avoid trapping dirt.
  • Start from the front and work your way back.
  • Secure straps and elastic hems tightly but avoid over-tightening.
  • Use additional tie-downs in windy areas.
  • Remove the cover periodically to air out and prevent moisture buildup.

Maintenance and Cleaning of RV Covers

  • Wash covers with mild soap and water; avoid harsh chemicals.
  • Allow covers to dry completely before storage.
  • Store in a cool, dry place when not in use.
  • Inspect regularly for tears or wear and repair promptly.

Troubleshooting Common RV Cover Issues

  • Moisture buildup: Use breathable covers or add vents.
  • Tears or rips: Patch small holes with repair kits.
  • Loose fit: Adjust straps or consider a custom cover.
  • Mildew: Clean with vinegar solution and ensure proper drying.

Cost Analysis: What to Expect When Buying an RV Cover

  • Budget covers start around $100 but may lack durability.
  • Mid-range covers ($150-$250) offer balanced protection and longevity.
  • Premium custom-fit covers can exceed $300 but provide the best fit and materials.
  • Factor in replacement frequency and potential damage costs when budgeting.

Safety Considerations When Using RV Covers

  • Avoid covering hot engines or exhausts to prevent fire hazards.
  • Ensure covers do not obstruct vents or emergency exits.
  • Use reflective strips or markers if covering outdoors at night.
  • Secure covers to prevent wind damage or accidents.

Seasonal and Regional Considerations for RV Covers

  • In snowy regions, choose waterproof, heavy-duty covers with snow-shedding designs.
  • In hot, sunny climates, prioritize UV protection and breathability.
  • For coastal areas, salt and moisture-resistant materials are essential.
  • Consider seasonal storage covers for winterizing your RV.

Certifications and Standards for RV Covers

  • Look for covers meeting UV Protection Factor (UPF) standards.
  • Waterproof ratings such as IPX4 or higher ensure rain resistance.
  • Certifications for mildew resistance and fire retardancy add safety.
  • Check manufacturer compliance with environmental and material safety standards.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

  1. What size RV cover do I need?
    Measure your RV’s length, width, and height, then select a cover size that matches or slightly exceeds these dimensions.

  2. Can I use an RV cover in all weather conditions?
    Choose covers designed for your specific climate—waterproof for rain/snow, breathable for hot climates.

  3. How often should I replace my RV cover?
    Typically every 3-5 years, depending on material quality and exposure.

  4. Are custom-fit covers worth the extra cost?
    Yes, they provide better protection and fit, reducing wear and tear.

  5. Can I leave my RV cover on during travel?
    No, covers are for storage only and should be removed before driving.

  6. How do I prevent mold under the cover?
    Use breathable covers and ensure your RV is dry before covering.

  7. Are there covers specifically for motorhomes vs. trailers?
    Yes, many covers are designed for specific RV types.

  8. Can I wash my RV cover in a washing machine?
    Most covers are too large; hand washing with mild soap is recommended.

  9. What accessories do I need with an RV cover?
    Tie-down straps, storage bags, and repair kits are useful additions.

  10. Do RV covers protect against theft?
    They provide some deterrence but are not a security device.

  11. How do I store my RV cover when not in use?
    Fold it neatly and store in a dry, cool place away from direct sunlight.

  12. Can I use a tarp instead of an RV cover?
    Tarps offer less protection and can trap moisture, leading to damage.

  13. Are there eco-friendly RV covers?
    Yes, some brands use recycled or sustainable materials.

  14. How do I know if my RV cover is UV resistant?
    Check product specifications for UPF ratings or UV protection claims.

  15. What is the best way to secure an RV cover in windy areas?
    Use additional tie-downs and secure straps to anchor the cover firmly.
