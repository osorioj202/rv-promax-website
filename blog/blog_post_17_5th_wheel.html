What Is a 5th Wheel Trailer? Complete Overview

A 5th wheel trailer is a type of RV trailer designed to be towed by a pickup truck equipped with a special hitch mounted in the truck bed. Unlike traditional travel trailers that connect to a bumper hitch, the 5th wheel hitch sits over the rear axle of the truck, providing better weight distribution and improved towing stability.

Key Features of 5th Wheel Trailers:

  • Hitch Location: Mounted in the truck bed, typically over or slightly in front of the rear axle.
  • Size & Layout: Generally larger and more spacious than conventional travel trailers, often with multiple slide-outs.
  • Weight Capacity: Can range from 7,000 to over 20,000 pounds GVWR (Gross Vehicle Weight Rating).
  • Living Space: Offers residential-style amenities including full kitchens, bathrooms, and separate bedrooms.
  • Towing Stability: Reduced sway and improved maneuverability compared to bumper-pull trailers.

Who Should Consider a 5th Wheel?

  • Full-time RVers needing ample living space.
  • Weekend campers with heavy-duty trucks.
  • Families or groups requiring multiple sleeping areas.
  • Anyone prioritizing towing safety and comfort.

Buying Guide: What to Look for in a 5th Wheel Trailer and Hitch

Hitch Features

  • Weight Capacity: Match the hitch rating to your trailer’s GVWR.
  • Jaw Type: Dual jaw hitches offer better security than single jaw.
  • Adjustability: Height adjustment ensures proper leveling.
  • Installation: Bolt-on vs. rail-mounted options depending on your truck.

Trailer Size and Layout

  • Length: Typically 20 to 40 feet; consider your towing vehicle’s capacity.
  • Slide-Outs: More slide-outs mean more living space but add weight.
  • Floor Plan: Look for layouts that fit your lifestyle (e.g., bunk beds, office space).

Compatibility

  • Ensure your truck’s bed size and payload capacity match the hitch and trailer.
  • Check pin box compatibility for smooth connection.

Safety Features

  • Integrated breakaway systems.
  • Emergency brake controllers.
  • Proper lighting and wiring harnesses.

Common Problems with 5th Wheels and How to Fix Them

Problem: Trailer Sway

  • Solution: Use a weight distribution hitch or sway control devices; ensure proper tongue weight (15-25% of trailer weight).

Problem: Hitch Noise or Looseness

  • Solution: Regularly lubricate hitch components; tighten bolts and check for wear.

Problem: Uneven Tire Wear

  • Solution: Check alignment and tire pressure; balance tires regularly.

Problem: Landing Gear Failure

  • Solution: Inspect electrical connections; manually operate gear to check for jams.

Professional Tips and Best Practices for 5th Wheel Owners

  • Always perform a pre-trip inspection of hitch, tires, and lights.
  • Use a high-quality torque wrench to tighten hitch bolts to manufacturer specs.
  • Invest in a portable 5th wheel scale to monitor weight distribution.
  • Practice backing up your 5th wheel in an open area to build confidence.
  • Store your 5th wheel with hitch cover and wheel covers to protect from elements.

Step-by-Step Installation Guide for a 5th Wheel Hitch

  1. Prepare Your Truck Bed: Clean and clear the bed; remove any bed liners if necessary.
  2. Install Mounting Rails: Position rails according to manufacturer specs; use a tape measure for accuracy.
  3. Secure Rails: Drill pilot holes and bolt rails to the truck frame using supplied hardware.
  4. Attach Hitch Head: Mount the hitch head to the rails; ensure it locks securely.
  5. Test Fit: Lower the hitch to the correct height and test with your trailer’s king pin.
  6. Final Checks: Tighten all bolts, lubricate moving parts, and test locking mechanism.

Maintenance, Troubleshooting, and Care for Your 5th Wheel

  • Lubricate Hitch: Use high-quality grease on jaws and pivot points every 3,000 miles.
  • Inspect Wiring: Check for corrosion or loose connections monthly.
  • Clean Landing Gear: Remove dirt and debris; test operation regularly.
  • Tire Care: Rotate tires every 6,000 miles; maintain recommended PSI.
  • Winter Storage: Use hitch covers and disconnect batteries to prevent damage.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: Can I tow a 5th wheel with any pickup truck?
A1: No, your truck must have sufficient payload capacity and a properly installed 5th wheel hitch.

Q2: How much does a 5th wheel hitch weigh?
A2: Typically between 80 and 150 pounds depending on the model.

Q3: Is a 5th wheel easier to tow than a bumper pull trailer?
A3: Yes, due to better weight distribution and reduced sway.

Q4: Can I install a 5th wheel hitch myself?
A4: Yes, if you have the right tools and follow instructions carefully, but professional installation is recommended.

Q5: How often should I lubricate my 5th wheel hitch?
A5: Every 3,000 miles or at least twice a year.

Q6: What is the ideal tongue weight for a 5th wheel?
A6: Between 15% and 25% of the trailer’s total weight.

Q7: Are there safety devices I should add to my 5th wheel setup?
A7: Yes, breakaway switches, sway control bars, and weight distribution systems improve safety.

Q8: How do I winterize my 5th wheel?
A8: Drain water systems, disconnect batteries, and cover the hitch and tires.
