What Are Motorhome Awnings? A Technical Overview

Motorhome awnings are retractable or fixed coverings attached to the side of an RV, designed to provide shade and protection from the elements. They extend outward to create a sheltered outdoor area, enhancing your camping experience by offering relief from sun, rain, and wind.

Types of Motorhome Awnings

  • Manual Awnings: Operated by hand crank or pull strap. Affordable and simple but require physical effort.
  • Electric Awnings: Powered by a motor, extend and retract at the push of a button. More convenient but pricier.
  • Slide-Out Awnings: Specifically designed to cover slide-out sections of the RV, protecting them from weather and debris.
  • Full-Length Awnings: Cover the entire side of the motorhome, providing maximum shade and space.

Materials and Construction

  • Fabric: Typically made from acrylic, vinyl, or polyester. Acrylic offers UV resistance and breathability; vinyl is waterproof but can trap heat.
  • Frame: Aluminum or steel frames provide structural support. Aluminum is lightweight and corrosion-resistant.
  • Hardware: Includes arms, rollers, and mounting brackets, often powder-coated for durability.

Comprehensive Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Motorhome Awning

1. Measure Your RV

  • Measure the length of the side where the awning will be installed.
  • Consider the height and clearance for proper extension.

2. Determine Your Usage

  • Frequent campers may prefer electric awnings for convenience.
  • Occasional users might opt for manual awnings to save cost.

3. Material Considerations

  • Acrylic fabrics offer breathability and UV protection.
  • Vinyl fabrics provide waterproofing but can trap heat.

4. Size and Coverage

  • Choose an awning width that matches or slightly exceeds your RV side length.
  • Consider extension length for desired outdoor space.

5. Installation Type

  • Factory-installed awnings offer seamless integration.
  • Aftermarket awnings require professional or DIY installation.

6. Additional Features

  • LED lighting for nighttime use.
  • Wind and rain sensors for automatic retraction.
  • Remote control operation.

Troubleshooting Common Motorhome Awning Issues

Problem Possible Cause Solution
Awning won’t extend Motor failure or power issue Check power supply, reset motor, replace fuse
Awning fabric tearing Wind damage or wear Patch small tears, replace fabric if severe
Awning won’t retract Obstruction or mechanical jam Clear debris, lubricate moving parts
Frame rusting Exposure to moisture Clean and apply rust-resistant coating

Expert Solutions

  • Regularly inspect awning arms and fabric for damage.
  • Use manufacturer-approved lubricants on moving parts.
  • Store awning fully retracted during high winds or storms.

Professional Tips and Best Practices

  • Always retract awnings during strong winds or heavy rain to prevent damage.
  • Clean awning fabric with mild soap and water; avoid harsh chemicals.
  • Use awning tie-downs or support poles for added stability.
  • Schedule annual professional inspections for electric awnings.
  • Consider adding a protective awning cover during off-season storage.

Step-by-Step Installation Instructions for Manual Awnings

  1. Gather Tools: Drill, level, measuring tape, screwdriver, mounting brackets.
  2. Measure and Mark: Determine mounting height and mark bracket locations.
  3. Install Brackets: Secure brackets to RV side using appropriate fasteners.
  4. Attach Awning: Mount awning roller tube to brackets.
  5. Secure Arms: Attach support arms and adjust pitch.
  6. Test Operation: Extend and retract awning to ensure smooth function.
  7. Seal Mounts: Apply sealant around brackets to prevent leaks.

For electric awnings, professional installation is recommended due to wiring requirements.

Maintenance and Care Advice

  • Clean fabric every 3-6 months with soft brush and mild detergent.
  • Inspect frame and hardware for corrosion or damage.
  • Lubricate moving parts annually.
  • Store awning dry to prevent mold and mildew.
  • Replace worn or damaged parts promptly to extend lifespan.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: Can I install a motorhome awning myself?

Yes, manual awnings can often be installed by experienced DIYers with proper tools. Electric awnings usually require professional installation due to wiring.

Q2: How long do motorhome awnings last?

With proper care, awnings typically last 5-10 years depending on material quality and usage.

Q3: Are electric awnings worth the extra cost?

Electric awnings offer convenience and ease of use, especially for frequent campers or those with mobility concerns.

Q4: How do I clean and maintain my awning?

Use mild soap and water, avoid harsh chemicals, and allow the fabric to dry completely before retracting.

Q5: What size awning should I buy?

Match the awning width to your RV side length and consider extension length based on desired outdoor space.
