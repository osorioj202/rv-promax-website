Why RV Covers Are Essential for Every RV Owner

RV covers serve as a protective shield against weather, dirt, UV rays, and other hazards that can degrade your vehicle’s exterior and interior over time. Here’s why investing in a quality RV cover is a smart move:

  • Weather Protection: Rain, snow, hail, and wind can cause rust, corrosion, and water damage.
  • UV Ray Defense: Prolonged sun exposure fades paint, cracks seals, and damages rubber components.
  • Pest Prevention: Covers help keep birds, rodents, and insects from nesting in your RV.
  • Dirt and Debris Shield: Dust, tree sap, and bird droppings can stain and erode surfaces.
  • Preserves Resale Value: A well-maintained exterior means higher resale or trade-in value.
  • Convenience: Covers reduce cleaning frequency and maintenance costs.

What You Need to Know About RV Covers

Types of RV Covers

  • Universal Covers: Designed to fit a wide range of RV sizes and shapes; budget-friendly but may not offer a perfect fit.
  • Custom-Fit Covers: Tailored to your RV’s exact dimensions for maximum protection and ease of use.
  • Indoor Covers: Lightweight covers for dust protection when stored inside garages or storage units.
  • Outdoor Covers: Heavy-duty, weather-resistant covers designed to withstand harsh outdoor conditions.

Materials Used

  • Polyester: Lightweight, water-resistant, and affordable; good for mild climates.
  • Polypropylene: Durable and breathable; prevents moisture buildup.
  • Vinyl: Waterproof but less breathable; can trap moisture if not ventilated.
  • Multi-layered Fabrics: Combine waterproofing and breathability for superior protection.

Key Features to Consider

  • UV protection rating
  • Waterproof and breathable fabric
  • Reinforced seams and tie-downs
  • Mold and mildew resistance
  • Ease of installation and removal
  • Storage bag included

Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right RV Cover

1. Measure Your RV Accurately

Measure length, width, and height from the widest points. Check manufacturer specs for slide-outs and accessories.

2. Consider Your Storage Environment

  • Outdoor storage requires waterproof, UV-resistant covers.
  • Indoor storage can use lighter, dust-proof covers.

3. Material and Breathability

Choose breathable fabrics to prevent moisture buildup and mold. Waterproof layers are essential for rain and snow protection.

4. Fit and Ease of Use

Custom-fit covers offer the best protection but cost more. Universal covers are easier to find but may flap in wind.

5. Additional Features

Look for reinforced corners, tie-down straps, vents, and storage bags.

6. Budget

Prices range from $150 to $800+. Balance cost with durability and protection needs.

Common Problems with RV Covers and How to Solve Them

Problem: Moisture Trapped Under Cover

Solution: Use breathable covers with vents to allow airflow and prevent mold.

Problem: Cover Flapping in Wind

Solution: Secure with adjustable straps and reinforced tie-downs.

Problem: Cover Tears or Wears Quickly

Solution: Choose heavy-duty, multi-layer fabrics and avoid dragging the cover on rough surfaces.

Problem: Difficult Installation

Solution: Opt for custom-fit covers and practice installation with a helper.

Problem: UV Damage Despite Cover

Solution: Ensure the cover has a high UV protection rating and replace worn covers promptly.

Expert Tips and Best Practices for RV Cover Use

  • Always clean and dry your RV before covering to prevent trapping dirt and moisture.
  • Use a cover designed for your specific RV class and size.
  • Remove the cover periodically to inspect for damage or moisture.
  • Store the cover in a dry place when not in use.
  • Use additional accessories like wheel covers and vent protectors for comprehensive protection.
  • Avoid covering your RV when it’s wet or snow-covered to prevent mold growth.

How to Install and Use Your RV Cover

  1. Prepare Your RV: Clean and dry the exterior thoroughly.
  2. Unfold the Cover: Lay it out flat near the RV.
  3. Start at the Front: Place the cover over the front, aligning it with the RV’s shape.
  4. Pull Over the Roof: Gently pull the cover over the roof and down the sides.
  5. Secure the Cover: Use built-in straps, buckles, and elastic hems to fasten the cover tightly.
  6. Check for Proper Fit: Adjust straps and hems to prevent flapping.
  7. Store Accessories: Keep the storage bag handy for when you remove the cover.

Maintenance and Care for Your RV Cover

  • Regular Cleaning: Wash the cover with mild soap and water; avoid harsh chemicals.
  • Dry Thoroughly: Always dry the cover completely before storage to prevent mildew.
  • Inspect for Damage: Check seams, straps, and fabric for wear and repair as needed.
  • Proper Storage: Fold and store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
  • Avoid Sharp Objects: Keep the cover away from sharp edges or branches that can tear fabric.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: How often should I replace my RV cover?
A: Typically every 3-5 years, depending on exposure and material quality.

Q2: Can I use an RV cover in winter?
A: Yes, but ensure it’s designed for all-season use and properly secured against snow and ice.

Q3: Will an RV cover protect against hail damage?
A: Covers provide some protection against minor hail but may not prevent damage from large hailstones.

Q4: How do I prevent mold under the cover?
A: Use breathable covers with vents and avoid covering a wet RV.

Q5: Can I leave my RV cover on while driving?
A: No, covers are for storage only and should be removed before driving.

Q6: Are custom-fit covers worth the extra cost?
A: Yes, they offer better protection, fit securely, and are easier to install.

Q7: How do I measure my RV for a cover?
A: Measure length, width, and height at the widest points, including slide-outs and accessories.
